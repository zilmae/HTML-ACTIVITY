Editor's Note

School life in the digital age has changed the way students learn, communicate, and organize their schoolwork. With tools like laptops, tablets, learning apps, and online platforms, students can access information quickly, collaborate with classmates, and submit assignments digitally. Lessons are no longer limited to textbooks—videos, simulations, and interactive activities make learning more engaging and hands-on.

I believe that digital technology can make school life more exciting and efficient if used responsibly. It gives students the chance to explore new ways of learning, complete projects faster, and communicate more easily with peers and teachers. But it’s also important to stay disciplined and avoid distractions, so that technology supports learning instead of hindering it.
