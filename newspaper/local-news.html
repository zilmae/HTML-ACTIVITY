Road Improvement Project Begins

Infrastructure upgrades aim to improve traffic flow

The local government of Antipolo recently started a road improvement project in Barangay Mayamot. The goal is to reduce traffic congestion and improve safety for pedestrians and motorists. Residents have long requested better road conditions because potholes and narrow lanes have caused problems for many years.

According to local officials, the project will widen the road and add proper drainage systems. Construction is expected to take several months but will benefit thousands of residents who travel through the area daily. Community members expressed hope that the project will make commuting safer and faster.

Barangay Clean-Up Drive

Volunteers join forces to improve the environment

Residents of Barangay Mayamot recently participated in a community clean-up drive organized by local leaders. Students, parents, and volunteers gathered early in the morning to clean streets, canals, and public spaces. The activity aimed to promote environmental awareness and community cooperation.

Participants collected bags of trash and recyclable materials. Barangay officials said similar activities will continue every month to encourage responsible waste management. Many residents believe these programs help build unity and pride in the community.
