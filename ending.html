The End



After that night, the villagers finally believed the legend. They no longer ignored the wisdom of the elders and learned to respect the dangers of the unseen world. People avoided the forest after sunset, traveled in groups, and protected their homes with prayers, charms, and symbols believed to ward off evil. The story of the Tiyanak was told again and again, not only to frighten children but to teach everyone a lesson about caution, wisdom, and listening to those who came before. Even now, when a baby’s cry echoes in the forest at night, the villagers remember the legend—and they stay safely behind their doors.
